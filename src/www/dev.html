Use the drop-down menu above to select the type of Grid and type of Entropy you wish to use for the grid.

As a minimum, you will want to download two grids:

  1. A Pattern Grid (for mapping out/drawing your preferred 11 or 23-cell patterns or co-ordinates)
  2. An Entropy Grid, featuring either Words, Numbers, Indices and/or Hexadecimals.

Most users will opt for Word Grids, which produces the first four letters of each BIP39 seed word in a randomised order using the Fisher-Yates Shuffle algorithm running locally in your browser. When you hit "GENERATE GRID", your chosen grid(s) - formatted in a 2-page PDF - will be sent to your downloads folder.

We offer users the ability to employ two types of entropy when generating their Entropy Grids - 128-bit and 19580-bit:

  • The 128-bit option provides users with deterministic entropy grid reproducibility - i.e. the ability to backup grids in plain text as 12 words and regenerate later them using the Entropy Grid Generator.
  • The 19580-bit option provides users with the maximum level of entropy. Because of the randomness of these grids, regeneration is not possible. These grids must therefore be saved and stored safely as there is no way to regenerate them later from backup words.

You have chosen to generate an Entropy Grid from your own passphrase. Entropy is about RANDOMNESS! There is no randomness involved in creating your Pattern Grid as you need to make a pattern that is not random and easy to remember. This means that all the entropy for your seed should come from the Entropy Grid (hence the name!)

Cryptographers highly recommend (perhaps even insist on) a minimum of 128-bits of entropy to generate Bitcoin seeds. We have included a tool that will calculate the minimum potential entropy of your passphrase, based on the length and set of characters used. This is NOT a measure of the entropy in your passphrase and how you generate randomness is up to you when using this advanced feature.

We recommend the EFF's Diceware Long Word List if you wish to do this yourself. Note that you will need to generate a 10 word passphrase to have at least 128 bits of entropy. Our built in solution uses 12 words from Bitcoin's, much shorter, BIP39 word list from 128 bits of entropy in the same way that wallets make seeds

If you generate your own 128-bit entropy and input your passphrase here, the tool may say that your potential entropy is higher. You can safely ignore it in this case, safe in the knowledge that you have generated sufficient entropy on your own.

Note well that the tool will not generate an Entropy Grid if the potential entropy of your passphrase is below 128 bits. This is for your own protection and for the integrity of the Border Wallets concept as a whole.

Entropy Grids generated using the Deterministic (128-bit entropy) option feature a 12-word Regeneration Phrase. This may seem counter-intuitive to the concept of Border Wallets, but the ability to save 12-words in plain text can serve an important backup and/or decoy purpose for some users.

Whilst this Regeneration tab does allow users to enter their own passphrase to generate (and thus regenerate) grid entropy based on their own choice of words, this is strongly discouraged - humans are very bad at entropy and your funds may be at risk if you do this.

NB: Entropy Grids which are generated using the Maximum Entropy (19580-bit) option are not able to be regenerated using similar words (and therefore do not include a Regeneration Phrase when produced).
